About Steve Ano

A dedicated community leader committed to transforming Busali

Who is Steve Ano?

Steve Ano is a lifelong Busali resident and community organizer with 10+ years of experience working with youth, farmers, and small business owners.

He has led successful initiatives in skill training, cooperative management, and community development. Steve believes that good governance starts with listening to the people.

Why Steve? Because Busali deserves a leader who walks with them, not above them. Steve's track record speaks for itself.

🏆 Youth Empowerment & Jobs

The Plan:

  • Busali Youth Skills Hub: Carpentry, welding, tailoring, digital skills training—funded by county youth budget
  • Boda Boda Saccos: Support registration, insurance, and working capital for riders
  • Job Placement: Link every trained youth to county contracts and private sector opportunities
  • Starter Toolkits: Free tools for youth who complete training to start their own businesses

Timeline: Launch Q3 2027 | Budget: KES 15M from county allocation

👩 Women Empowerment

The Plan:

  • Market Infrastructure: Build/upgrade 10 market sheds with electricity, water taps, and solar lighting
  • Table Banking SACCO: Low-interest loans for women entrepreneurs and traders
  • Girls Bursaries: 500 girls in secondary school supported annually
  • AGPO Priority: Women groups get first access to 30% of county government tenders

Timeline: Begin Q4 2027 | Budget: KES 20M

🌽 Agribusiness & Farmers

The Plan:

  • Subsidized Inputs: Seeds and fertilizer delivered on time at reduced cost
  • Aggregation Center: Busali maize and dairy aggregation hub for better prices
  • Value Addition Training: Postharvest processing, storage, packaging skills
  • Cold Room Facility: Cooperative cold room to end post-harvest losses

Timeline: Foundation Q3 2027 | Budget: KES 25M

🎓 Quality Education

The Plan:

  • Transparent Bursaries: Clear criteria, public accountability, no favoritism
  • ECD Classrooms: Upgrade 15 Early Childhood Development centers with furniture and books
  • School Feeding: Partner with churches and NGOs for daily meals to prevent hunger-induced dropout
  • Infrastructure: Desks, toilets, labs, and learning materials in all schools

Timeline: Immediate Q1 2027 | Budget: KES 18M

🏥 Affordable Healthcare

The Plan:

  • Busali Health Center: Stock with essential drugs and maternity beds
  • NHIF Enrollment: Monthly registration drives in every village (target: 5,000 families)
  • Community Health Workers: Equipment and training for village health volunteers
  • Dispensary Upgrades: Support for private and mission health facilities

Timeline: Begin Q2 2027 | Budget: KES 12M

🚜 Roads & Water Access

The Plan:

  • Year-Round Road Maintenance: Grade and drain all feeder roads—no more impassable stretches
  • Boreholes: Sink 20 new boreholes in water-scarce areas
  • Rainwater Tanks: Install solar-powered tanks at schools, clinics, and dry villages
  • Community Taps: Public water points within walking distance of every household

Timeline: Year-long Q2–Q4 2027 | Budget: KES 30M
